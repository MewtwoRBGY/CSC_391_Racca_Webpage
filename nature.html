Not God
Name Century Core Claim Response Explanation
Arianism 3rd Century Teaches that Christ was made by God and is distinct from God 321, Arius denounced for heterodoxy and Arianism was condemned at Council of Nicea which made the Nicene Creed One of the most well known heresies, but held by little Christians today. According to legend, St. Nicholas slapped Arius at the Council of Nicea
Adoptionism 2nd Century Teaches that Christ was human that was merely adopted by God Condemned by many Church fathers such as Irenaus and Origien Held in the early Church by Ebionites, a judaizing Christian sect which followed the old Law.
Not Human
Name Century Core Claim Response Explanation
Docetism 2nd Century Teaches that Christ only appeared to be human and that his human appearance was an illusion. Condemned at the Council of Nicea in 325 Comes from the word for illusion. Sommetimes compared to the incorrect, fringe, modern theory that Christ never existed.
Monophysitism 5th Century Teaches that Christ had one nature, with his human absorbed into his divine Contradicts the earlier Council of Nicea and was condemned at Chalcedon following a delcaration from Pope Leo the Great Distinct from Miaphysitism which is held by the Oriental Orthodox which says that Christ is fully human and divine but only has one nature
Other
Name Century Core Claim Response Explanation
Nestoranism 5th Century Teaches that Christ was two seperate persons, one human and one divine Condemned at Chalcedon, flourished in some areas but died out by end of 14th century Reason behind title Theotokos for the Virgin Mary to indicate that Christ is one person and thus Mary is not only the Mother of the "human" Jesus
Apollinarianism 4th Century Teaches that Christ had a human body and soul but that his mind was replaced by the Divine Logos Condemned at Council of Constantinople in 381 William Lane Craig proposed a similar doctrine he calls Neo-Apollinarianism
Monothelitism 7th Century Teaches that Christ had two natures but only one will, His Divine Will After lifting of a Roman Imperial ban of discussing Christ's nature, it was condemned at the Third Council of Constantinople Also held by William Lane Craig and some other Protestants
