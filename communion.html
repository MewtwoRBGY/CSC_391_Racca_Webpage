Western Heresies
Name Century Core Claim Response Explanation
Berengarianism 11th Century Proto-Protestant movement that denied Transubstantion but that "Christ's divine attributes are shared by the eucharistic bread via his body" Berengar arrested five times for not recanting his doctrine Precursor to Protestant Reformation and also denied infant baptism and believed in a sort of form of sola scriptura
Consubstantiation 14th Century Teaches that the substance of Christ's Body and Blood present also with substance of the bread and wine Condemned by the Church and later again condemned at Council of Trent Often cited as Luther's conception of Communion
Petrobrusians 12th Century Rejects Transubstantion, that the Eucharist is a sacrifice, and the use of altars Attacked in a treatise by Blessed Peter the Venerable and the founder, Peter of Bruys, was killed by a Catholic mob by throwing him into a bonfire that he threw crosses into Precursor to Protestantism; also condemned infant baptism, prayers for the dead, cross veneration, and the Old Testament and epistles
